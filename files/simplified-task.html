Day 12: Hill Climbing Algorithm

You are given a heightmap of the surrounding area, represented as a grid with each square's elevation represented by a lowercase letter. Your task is to find the fewest steps required to move from your current position (S) to the location that should get the best signal (E). You can only move up, down, left, or right to a square with an elevation that is at most one higher than your current square.

Example:

Sabqponm
abcryxxl
accszExk
acctuvwj
abdefghi

Starting from the top-left corner, you need to find the shortest path to the goal (E). The symbols ^, v, <, and > indicate whether the path exits each square moving up, down, left, or right respectively. The . symbol marks unvisited squares.

Using the given heightmap, find the fewest steps required to reach the location that should get the best signal.
